Basics of Writing Hànzì

What Hanzi Are - Chinese and Cantonese Characters

Chinese characters aren't letters. Each is a unit of meaning (a morpheme). Many characters consist of:

  • Radical (部首) – gives meaning or category
  • Phonetic component – hints at pronunciation

Example:

妈(mā, mother)
=
女(female)
+
马(mǎ, horse - phonetic)

Stroke Order Rules

Stroke order is very important. It helps writing speed, recognition, dictionary lookup, and most importantly, it will help you to remember the characters better.

Basic rules:

  • Top ➜ Bottom
  • Left ➜ Right
  • Horizontal ➜ Vertical
  • Outside ➜ Inside
  • Inside ➜ Closing stroke last
  • Dots / small finishing strokes last

Examples:

三3 horizontal strokes: top ➜ middle ➜ bottom
口Draw outer frame first: left ➜ top ➜ right, then close bottom
中Top stroke ➜ outer frame ➜ center vertical line last

Basic Stroke Types

Chinese characters are built from basic strokes. Here are the main categories:

－横 (héng) = horizontal
|竖 (shù) = vertical
丿撇 (piě) = left diagonal
㇏捺 (nà) = right diagonal
丶点 (diǎn) = dot
㇀提 (tí) = upward
㇕折 (zhé) = turning

Radicals = Your Best Friend

Radicals help you guess meaning:

氵water radical ➜ wash, ocean, river
扌hand radical ➜ hit, clap, push
女female radical ➜ mom, sister, younger sister
口mouth radical ➜ tell, drink, eat
亻person radical ➜ stand, rest, act
讠speech radical ➜ say, speak, language

Learn about 50 common radicals and reading gets much easier.

Writing Tools & Tips

  • Use squared paper (田字格 grid).
  • Practice slowly with correct stroke order.
  • Memorize characters by chunks, not random strokes.
  • Don't draw — write with confidence and firm strokes.
  • Review often — repetition matters.
